Check your eligibility

We’ll ask you to prove you trained and qualified at a similar level to teachers trained in England.

To get help with eligibility, email us at email@digital.education.gov.uk

You will need

A degree

Your degree can be in any subject, but it must be equivalent to a UK bachelor's degree. Contact UK ENIC to check the status of your degree in the UK

A teacher training qualification

Your teacher training needs to be of a similar standard to teacher training in England. This usually means a 3-year undergraduate teaching degree, or a 1-year postgraduate teacher training course.

Your training should include substantial practical experience in the classroom. You must be qualified to teach all children aged between 5 and 16 (not just those with special educational needs or disabilities).

At least one school year of teaching experience after you qualified

You will need a minimum of 1 year working in a classroom, teaching groups of at least 4 children between the ages of 5 and 16.

Your experience can be gained in any type of school (for example, international, state, private or specialist provision).

Professional status as a teacher

You must be able to prove that you enjoy a professional status as a fully qualified teacher in the country where you trained.

You must have no restrictions on your teaching practice and a clean employment record as a teacher.

Knowledge of English

Your English language skills must meet British Council level B2, or IELTS 5.5 to 6.5.

You will not need to prove your English language skills if:

  • your degree was taught or researched in English
  • you are a citizen of:
    • Antigua and Barbuda
    • Australia
    • The Bahamas
    • Barbados
    • Belize
    • Canada
    • Dominica
    • Grenada
    • Guyana
    • Ireland
    • Jamaica
    • Malta
    • New Zealand
    • St Kitts and Nevis
    • St Lucia
    • St Vincent and the Grenadines
    • Trinidad and Tobago
    • USA

If you do need to prove your knowledge of English, you can sit a secure English language test with an approved test provider.

Knowledge of Maths

Your knowledge of maths must meet a standard equivalent to a UK grade 4 GCSE.

A GCSE (General Certificate of Secondary Education) is an academic qualification awarded for exams in England, usually taken at age 16. For help understanding UK qualifications and their international equivalents, contact UK ENIC.

Knowledge of science (to teach ages 5 to 11 only)

To apply for QTS to teach primary school age children (aged 5 to 11), your knowledge of science (physics, chemistry, biology or a combination of these subjects) must meet a standard equivalent to a UK grade 4 GCSE.

A GCSE (General Certificate of Secondary Education) is an academic qualification awarded for exams in England, usually taken at age 16. For help understanding UK qualifications and their international equivalents, contact UK ENIC.
