You're eligible to apply for qualified teacher status (QTS)

When you apply, you'll need to provide documents that support your application.

Documents you’ll need

You’ll need to provide proof of your:

  • identity
  • qualifications
  • English language proficiency
  • teaching work history
  • status as a recognised teacher

If your documents are not written in English, you’ll need to provide certified translations of them (at your own cost).

Certified translations

A certified translator must state (on the translated document or a separate certification page) that it’s a true, accurate and correct copy of the original.

Each translated document must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

Finding a certified translator

You can find a professional translator through the Institute of Translating and Interpreting (ITI) (opens in new tab). All listed translators have been certified by the ITI and are qualified to complete translations.

Alternatively, some universities may be able to provide certified translation. You could search for universities with a language department and contact them for advice.

Completing the application

If we ask for a document you do not have yet, you can save your progress. You’ll need to complete your application within 6 months of starting it.

We’ll sign you out of the service if you’re inactive for 60 minutes.

If you wish, you can review detailed application requirements before starting your application.
