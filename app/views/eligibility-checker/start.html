Check if you can apply for qualified teacher status (QTS) in England

What this service does

We will assess the qualifications and teaching experience you gained outside England to decide if we can award you with qualified teacher staus (QTS). The decision we make will only be based on whether you meet our assessment criteria.

What QTS is

Qualified teacher status is a legal requirement to teach in many schools in England. It is a status that most schools prefer their teachers to have. Find out more about QTS.

Having QTS does not guarantee you a job or give you the right to work in England. You'll still need to apply for a teaching job seperately and have the correct visa or status.

Who can apply

This service is for qualified teachers who were trained to teach outside of England and want to teach in English schools.

  • an undergraduate degree equivalent to a UK bachelors degree
  • completed teacher training equivalent to a level 6 qualification (link)
  • qualifications to show you can teach children aged between 5 and 16
  • at least one school year (9 months) of experience employed as a teacher
  • been recognised as a professional teacher in the country where you qualified, and have no conditions or restrictions on your teaching practice

Depending on the country that you trained to teach in, you may be required to have:

  • qualifications to show you can teach children aged between 11 and 16
  • a teaching specialism in a subject that we have a shortage of teachers for

If you trained in England or Wales, or have not yet qualified, learn more about other routes to getting QTS.

! Warning The Department for Education (DfE) does not employ teachers directly. To find a teaching job, you must apply to individual schools in England.
