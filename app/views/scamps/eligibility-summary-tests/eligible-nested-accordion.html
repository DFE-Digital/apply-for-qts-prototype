You’re eligible to apply for qualified teacher status (QTS) in England

Preparing to apply

When you apply, you'll need to provide evidence that supports your application by uploading scans, photos, or original files of documents.

What we’ll ask for

You’ll need to provide proof of your:

  • Identity
  • qualifications
  • English language ability
  • work history
  • status as a recognised teacher

About certified translations

If your documents are not written in English, you’ll need to provide certified translations (at your own cost) for qualifications, transcripts, and proof that you're recognised as a teacher.

A certified translator must state (on the translated document or a separate certification page) that it’s a true, accurate and correct copy of the original.

Each one must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

We cannot recommend translation providers. You may want to search for translators who are members of the Institute of Translating and Interpretation (opens in a new tab). Sometimes a university’s language department can also help.

Starting your application

You can start your application straight away. We’ll explain what you need at each section of your application.

If we ask for a document you do not have yet, you can save your progress. You’ll need to complete your application within 6 months of starting it.

Alternatively, you can review the full requirements first, before starting your application.

Review the full requirements

1. Proof of identity

You must provide either a:

  • passport (must not have expired)
  • birth certificate
  • national identity card that shows your date of birth and nationality (must not have expired)

2. Proof of qualifications

You must hold a teaching qualification that’s equivalent to a UK bachelor degree (‘level 6’) or higher.

Check you hold a qualification that is equivalent or higher than a level 6.

We'll need to understand how much of your qualification was focussed on developing practical teaching skills.

You’ll need to provide evidence of your teaching qualification:

  • certificate (this might be your university degree, or other qualification)
  • transcript (or diploma supplement)

    • Trinidad and Tobago
    • USA
    • England
    • Scotland
    • Northern Ireland
    • Wales

    You’ll need to provide your passport or official identification documents for that country as proof.

    If you’re not exempt by birth or citizenship, you must be able to provide evidence of your English language ability in one of the following ways:

  • If you studied in an exempt country

    Upload the transcript from your teaching qualification or university degree (which you can request from the institution where you studied). Check the exempt countries listed in ‘Born in or have citizenship of an exempt country’.

  • If you studied in a country that’s NOT exempt

    If your teaching qualification or university degree was taught exclusively in the English language you can upload an official document, which is sometimes called a Medium of Instruction (MOI). You can request this from the institution where you studied.

  • Provide an approved English language certificate

    If you cannot provide one of the documents described in 1,2 or 3, you'll need a B2 level (opens in new tab) Secure English Language Test (SELT) from one of these approved providers:

    • Pearson: we can accept ‘PTE Academic UKVI’ or ‘PTE Home’
    • PSI Services: we can accept ‘Skills for English UKVI’
    • IELTS SELT Consortium: we can accept ‘IELTS for UKVI’ or ‘IELTS Life Skills’
    • LanguageCert: we can accept ‘LanguageCert International ESOL SELT’
    • Trinity College London: we can accept ‘Secure English Language Tests for UKVI’ – Integrated Skills in English (ISE) or Graded Examinations in Spoken English (GESE)

    The test must be awarded within the last 2 years before the date of your application.

    Find an approved provider (opens in new tab) if you need to get an English language certificate.

4. Proof of your work history

You need to show you’ve been employed as a teacher in a school for at least 9 months or more.

The teaching roles you include as proof can be from working in any country but must have occurred after becoming qualified to teach.

You’ll need to provide the name and email address of a contact who can confirm that during your professional work experience you:

  • worked unsupervised with children aged between 11 and 16 years
  • were solely responsible for planning, preparing and delivering lessons to at least 4 students at a time
  • were solely responsible for assessing and reporting on the progress of those students

We’ll contact the references you’ve provided to verify your work history before we award QTS.

6. Certified translations

If your documents are not written in English, you must provide certified translations (at your own cost) for your:

  • qualifications
  • transcripts
  • proof that you’re recognised as a teacher

You do not need translations for ID or change of name documents.

About certified translations

Translations must be completed by a certified translator, who must state (on the document or a separate certification page) that the translation is a true, accurate and correct copy of the original.

Each one must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

Finding a certified translator

We cannot recommend translation providers. You may want to explore translators who are members of the Institute of Translating and Interpretation (opens in a new tab). Sometimes a university’s language department can also help.

New criteria will apply to applications made from 1 February 2023.

If you do not submit your application before this date you’ll need to submit a new application under the new criteria.
