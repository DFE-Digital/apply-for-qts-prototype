You’re eligible to apply for qualified teacher status (QTS) in England

Preparing to apply

When you apply, you'll need to provide evidence that supports your application by uploading scans, photos, or original files of documents.

What we’ll ask for

You’ll need to provide proof of your:

  • Identity
  • qualifications
  • English language ability
  • work history
  • status as a recognised teacher

About certified translations

If your documents are not written in English, you’ll need to provide certified translations (at your own cost) for qualifications, transcripts, and proof that you're recognised as a teacher.

A certified translator must state (on the translated document or a separate certification page) that it’s a true, accurate and correct copy of the original.

Each one must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

We cannot recommend translation providers. You may want to search for translators who are members of the Institute of Translating and Interpretation (opens in a new tab). Sometimes a university’s language department can also help.

Starting your application

You can start your application straight away. We’ll explain what you need at each section of your application.

If we ask for a document you do not have yet, you can save your progress. You’ll need to complete your application within 6 months of starting it.

Alternatively, you can review the full requirements first, before starting your application.

Review the full requirements.

Apply for QTS
