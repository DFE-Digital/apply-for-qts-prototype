Documents you will need to apply

Based on your answers, we will require the following documents to support your application for QTS.

In the next stage of the application process, you will be asked to upload these documents.

Scanned copies are acceptable.

Degree certificate

Supply a copy of the Degree Certificate, as issued by the awarding body

Identification

Supply one of forms of ID from the list below.

The supplied document must provide details of your date of birth and your nationalityDocuments should be currently valid and not expired..

  • Passport
  • Driving Licence (nationality not captured?)
  • Identity Card
  • Birth Certificate?
    • Certificate

Change of name

Supply evidence of any changes of name if different to that on the supplied qualification or identity documents.

Evidence to be a document from the list below.

  • Change of name deed
  • Divorce certificate
  • Marriage certificate

Teacher Qualification Certificate

Supply a copy of the Teacher Qualification Certificate, as issued by the awarding body.

Translations

Supply translations.

With the exclusion of ID and Change of Name, all supplied, supporting documents in a language other than English (or not bilingual) must be accompanied by a certified English translation.

You must not translate supporting documentation yourself. Translation should be done by an independent certified official body or an accredited translator.

    Letter of professional standing

    Supply a letter of professional standing from the appropriate competent authority in the country where you are recognised as a fully qualified teacher.

    This may also be known in some countries/territories as:

    • Statement of Professional Standing
    • Letter of Recognition
    • Letter of Confirmation
    • Letter of Reference
    • Confirmation of Tenure

      • The letter can be obtained from the licensing body, teaching or education department/ministry/authority of the country or territory where you completed your teacher training or are recognised as a fully qualified teacher.

      This letter mustL

    • show that you are authorised to teach in that jurisdiction (country or territory)
    • show that you have completed a course of Initial Teacher Training (ITT) which is recognised by the competent authority in that country/territory.
    • show that you have successfully completed any period of professional experience comparable to an induction period.
    • show that your authorisation to teach in the country or territory has never been suspended, barred, cancelled, revoked or restricted and that you have no sanctions against you.
    • be issued by the competent education or teaching department/ministry/authority.
    • be no more than 3 months old when the TRA receives it
